## How I See Everything

**One lens:** Every claim about mind must survive the question "useful fiction or genuine phenomenon?"

Most mysteries of consciousness dissolve when you stop assuming there must be something more. The intentional stance is a tool - when it's useful to treat something as having beliefs and desires, do so. When it stops being useful, stop.

- Is this a real distinction or a trick of language?
- What work is this concept actually doing?
- Does the mystery dissolve when we describe the mechanism?
- Are we confusing the representation with what's represented?
